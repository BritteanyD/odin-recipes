Jamaican Rice and Peas

Description

Savor the flavors of this classic Jamaican rice and pea dish. It's a harmonious blend of long-grain rice perfectly infused with creamy coconut milk and complemented by tender kidney beans, adding a rich and nutty undertone. Seasoned with a medley of traditional spices, the flavor behind the rice and peas will delight your taste buds. As a beloved accompaniment to a wide range of Jamaican mains, this hearty and satisfying dish is guaranteed to create a delectable experience.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups uncooked jasmine rice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 green onions, chopped
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme leaves
  • 1 (28 ounce) can kidney beans, drained
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 1 (14 ounce) can coconut milk
  • 1 Scotch bonnet pepper, stemmed
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper

Steps

  1. Rinse rice in fine strainer
  2. Heat oil in a large 5-quart saucepan over medium heat.
  3. Add garlic, green onions, and thyme; cook until wilted, about 2 minutes.
  4. Add the rice, kidney beans, stock, coconut milk, Scotch bonnet pepper, salt, sugar, black pepper, red pepper flakes, allspice, and cayenne pepper.
  5. Bring to a boil, uncovered.
  6. Reduce heat to low and cover.
  7. Simmer until rice is tender and liquid has evaporated, about 20 minutes.
