Jamaican Fried Snapper

Description

Prepare to indulge in the tantalizing flavors of Jamaican fried snapper, a mouthwatering seafood dish known for its vibrant and bold taste. The snapper is seasoned with a flavorful blend of spices before being fried to perfection, resulting in a crispy and golden-brown exterior. Every single succulent bite unveils a tender and flaky flesh that's infused with the aromatic essence of each ingredient. Served alongside traditional Jamaican accompaniments, this fried snapper dish offers a delightful combination of textures and tastes, embodying the spirit of Jamaica's rich cultural heritage and passion for creating extraordinary seafood dishes.

Ingredients

  • 1 (1 1/2 pound) whole red snapper, cleaned and scaled
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 quart vegetable oil for frying
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 white onion, sliced
  • 1/2 large carrot, peeled and cut into thin strips
  • 1/8 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 3/4 tablespoon salt
  • 1/4 habanero pepper, seeded and minced
  • 1 sprig fresh thyme, leave stripped
  • 1 allspice berry, cracked
  • 1 pinch brown sugar

Steps

  1. Pat fish dry; cut 3 small slits on each side of fish. Season all over with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat 1-quart of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until smoking.
  3. Fry fish in hot oil until browned and crisp, about 5 minutes per side. Remove fish and place on a paper towel-lined plate.
  4. Heat 1 teaspoon of oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion, carrot, and garlic; cook and stir for 1 to 2 minutes.
  5. Add vinegar, water, salt, habanero pepper, thyme, allspice, and sugar; continue cooking until liquid has reduced, about 5 minutes.
  6. Spoon onion mixture over fried fish to serve.
